The project

My role as a Game Engineer on "Remothered: Broken Porcelain," the sequel to the acclaimed "Remothered: Tormented Fathers" was a continuation of my journey in crafting immersive horror experiences.

Building upon the foundations laid by the original game, I had the opportunity to contribute to a project that aimed to evolve and refine the vintage horror mechanics while maintaining the eerie allure that fans had come to love.

Some of the features I personally worked on are:

  • Development of core game functionalities such as Gameplay Progression, Save system, Interactions, collectibles.
  • AI Development using Behaviour Trees, Navigation System, AI Perception and EQS
  • Integration with Steam Online Services, XBOX Online Services. This included Achievements, Stats, Presences and Auth.
  • Porting to consoles (XboxOne, PS4) optimizing code and integrating with Microsoft and Sony SKDs

    • In this capacity, my responsibilities extended beyond the mere technical aspects of game development.

    Collaborating with the creative team, I helped shape the evolution of gameplay mechanics that added depth and complexity to the horror experience.

    With a firm understanding of the series' distinctive style, I fine-tuned character movements, interactions, and environmental elements, ensuring that players encountered a seamless blend of tension and intrigue.

    This involved leveraging my expertise to optimize performance and deliver a polished gaming experience that retained the nostalgic horror essence.
